What is Sedentary Behaviour?

Sedentary behaviour involves sitting or lying down, (with the exception of sleeping). You can be sedentary at work, at home, when travelling or during leisure time.

Examples of sedentary behaviour include:

  • Sitting or lying down while watching television.
  • Sitting while driving a vehicle, or while travelling.
  • Sitting or lying down to read, study, write, or work at a desk or computer.

    • Sedentary behaviour is important

    Being sedentary for prolonged periods (over 30 minutes) can have a negative impact on our health. In the short term, sedentary behaviour can make us feel sluggish and tired. In the longer term sedentary behaviour can increase your risk of developing type 2 diabetes, cancer and cardiovascular disease.

    What’s remarkable is that small changes to sedentary behaviour can make a big difference not just too how you feel in the short term, but also to your long term health and wellness. Sedentary behaviour can also be broken up by simple light intensity activities such as standing or chair-based exercises.
