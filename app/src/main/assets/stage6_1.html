6: Physical Activity and consequences

TOPIC

Wherever you are in terms of physical activity, starting a plan of physical activity that’s right for you is one of the greatest achievements you can make. Well done on successfully making your weekly planner!

While some benefits take time, others often happen relatively quickly. You are using some tips and tricks that are likely to help you succeed in achieving your planned goals. See below for further details.

Short Term Benefits

  • Feeling more alert, more awake and more focused.
  • The release of ‘feel good’ chemicals in the brain, lifting your mood, reducing stress and anxiety and improving self-esteem.
  • Increasing your metabolic rate (how quickly you burn calories) even when resting.

Long Term Benefits

  • 1 month after establishing regular exercises you may see an increase in muscle mass and reduction in body fat.
  • 6 months after establishing regular exercise, your heart will have grown in size and become stronger and more efficient.
  • 12 months after establishing regular exercise, bone health and density will have improved, lowering your risk of fracture.
  • Improvements in mental health are also likely to have continued.
